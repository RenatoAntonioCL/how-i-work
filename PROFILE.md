# Profile

I'm a self-taught software developer based in Santiago, Chile.

I got into programming because I kept seeing new tools doing things automatically and thought: I can build that. I can automate my own problems — and other people's. That instinct drives everything I build.

What I care about isn't making things work. It's making things **last**. If the foundation is wrong, everything built on top is borrowed time.

---

## What sets me apart

Most developers stop when it works. I don't.

A system that works but can't be observed, scaled, or reasoned about isn't finished — it's a liability waiting to surface. I build for the next engineer (usually future me), not just the next demo. I document decisions, not just outcomes. I own the system end-to-end: design, implementation, deploy, monitoring, and post-mortem.

---

## What I value

**Foundations over features.** A strong core outlasts any roadmap.

**Resilience over speed.** Fast and fragile loses to slow and solid every time.

**Ownership without excuses.** If it's mine, it's mine completely — including when it breaks.

**Time efficiency over time quantity.** The goal is systems that run without constant intervention, not engineers who work around the clock.

**Zero recurring failures.** One mistake is learning. The same mistake twice is a process problem.

---

## How I work

I think by building. I iterate fast, document what I learn, and treat every decision as an ADR waiting to be written. I don't chase trends — I pick tools that solve the problem, and I'm willing to change them when something better fits.

I work alone today. I build as if there's already a team.

---

## What I won't do

I won't spend my time firefighting systems built on deficient foundations — especially when the same failures keep happening. I'd rather fix the root cause once than patch symptoms indefinitely.
